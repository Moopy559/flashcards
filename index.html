Can you provide an example of a complex digital project you've planned and managed from start to finish?

  • Situation: As Head of the Marketing department at ENERDRIVE | DOMETIC focused on UI/UX, I led and liaised with multiple cross-functional teams and contractors to ensure the successful re-engineering and design of the mobile app for our flagship product.
  • Task: The task was to conduct user research and develop a new app with several new customer support features, a new user-interface design, and also ensure it met the requirements of several internal teams that would be supporting the app and end-users post-launch from a product and tech-support perspective. Using a combination of Adobe Creative Suite and Figma, I designed wireframes and prototypes for the new app and received sign-off from all of the involved teams to ship into development. It was considered a massive team success and developed a unified direction for our core product that all internal teams could rally behind.

How do you prioritize tasks and allocate resources when managing multiple digital projects simultaneously?

  • I begin by mapping out all of the individual tasks associated with the projects into a project management software, such as Trello or Monday. I then start assessing the importance and urgency of each task based on strategic alignment, deadlines and impact on stakeholders. Anything with imminent deadlines or those directly tied to critical business objectives are given higher priority.
  • I’d then look at the availability of resources, including team members, budgets and technology for each task or project. I then assign skill sets and expertise accordingly. I’d also make sure that I’m transparent with my communication to stakeholders, regularly reviewing and adjusting my approach as I recognise that priorities and resources may be subject to change.

How do you ensure that business requirements for digital enhancements are accurately captured and translated into actionable plans?

  • I would begin by identifying the key stakeholders from various departments and engaging with them to understand their needs, objectives and also the pain points.
  • Throughout this process I would conduct workshops, brainstorming sessions or interviews as required to ensure that all of the relevant nuances leading to these requirements are understood.
  • I’d then meticulously document all requirements, ensuring they’re captured in a clear and concise manner. That documentation might include user stories, use cases and criteria to better illustrate the desired enhancements.
  • I then collaborate closely with developers and technical staff to translate those business requirements into actionable plans and project timelines. This would all be backed by clear and consistent communication.
  • Lastly, I would continuously seek feedback from stakeholders to identify any areas for improvement

  • Task: My task was to develop a strategic approach to vendor selection and management that would optimize value, quality, and reliability while mitigating risks associated with outsourcing critical components of our digital projects. This involved defining project requirements, identifying potential vendors, evaluating proposals, negotiating contracts, and maintaining effective communication throughout the project lifecycle.

Facilitated vendor meetings and interviews to clarify expectations, address questions, and ensure alignment between vendor capabilities and project requirements.
  • Result: As a result of my strategic approach to vendor selection and management, we were able to successfully procure goods and services for digital projects that met quality standards, budgetary requirements, and project timelines.
